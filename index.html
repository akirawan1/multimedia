How Coastal Cities Are Rewriting Climate Policy Street by Street

One multimedia article combining editorial investigation, documentary footage, and data visualization from six months of field reporting.

By Mira S. Halim | 12 Min Read | April 2026

Scroll to Continue

At dawn in the Harbor District, flood sirens are no longer extraordinary. They are part of the school commute, the produce market opening bell, and the city council's daily briefing. Across three coastal capitals, climate policy has shifted from distant megaproject renderings to block-by-block interventions that citizens can inspect, measure, and challenge in public.

"The cities making progress are not always spending the most, they are publishing clearer timelines and allowing residents to verify outcomes themselves."
